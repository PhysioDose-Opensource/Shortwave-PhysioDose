Description
Title: PhysioDose: Shortwave diathermy dose calculator for clinical, academic, and scientific use in musculoskeletal conditions

Description:

Despite the widespread clinical use of Shortwave Diathermy (SWD)—both in pulsed (pSWD) and continuous (CSWD) modes—a recurrent and significant methodological flaw in physical therapy literature is the incomplete reporting of technical parameters and the omission of precise dosimetric calculations. This critical lack of standardization hinders the reproducibility of interventions, compromises the comparative analysis of clinical trials, and obscures the dose-response relationship in musculoskeletal treatments.

PhysioDose was developed to address this critical methodological gap. This standalone, offline-ready application provides researchers and clinicians with a rigorous, evidence-based tool to compute the exact net thermal load delivered during SWD treatments. By inputting standard machine parameters (Peak Power, Pulse Width, Pulse Repetition Frequency, and Treatment Duration), the software accurately calculates the Duty Cycle, Mean Power (W), and Total Energy (kJ).

Furthermore, the application categorizes the estimated clinical effect (Athermal/Low, Mild Thermal, Moderate, or Vigorous) based on current scientific literature (Laufer et al., 2012; Masiero et al., 2019) and integrates clinical safety guidelines focused on patient thermal perception.

Academic and Educational Relevance:

Beyond its utility in clinical research, PhysioDose serves as an essential resource in academic and educational settings. It assists physical therapy students and novice practitioners in bridging the gap between theoretical physics and clinical application, allowing them to visualize how modifications in machine parameters directly impact the final therapeutic dose.

By incorporating a feature that generates standardized, ready-to-copy "Intervention Reports," the tool actively instills rigorous documentation habits. It trains future professionals to systematically record dosimetric data, thereby overcoming the traditional reporting shortcomings found in historical physical therapy literature.

Key Features:

Calculation of Duty Cycle, Mean Power, and Total Delivered Energy.

Support for both Pulsed (pSWD) and Continuous (CSWD) emission modes.

Evidence-based clinical interpretation of dosage.

Automated generation of standardized scientific intervention reports.

Zero-dependency, offline-ready HTML architecture ensuring long-term accessibility.
